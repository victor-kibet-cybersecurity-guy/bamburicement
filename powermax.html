Using Bamburi PowerMax Cement

Surface Preparation

For optimal results with Bamburi PowerMax Cement, proper surface preparation is critical. Ensure the substrate is stable and leveled. Remove any loose materials or existing weak concrete. Eliminate contaminants like dust or grease using a wire brush or pressure wash. This prepares the surface for a strong bond, ensuring durable and lasting structural performance.

Mix, pour, and build

  • Remove dust, dirt, and loose particles from the surface.
  • Clear away grease or oil residues completely.
  • Ensure the surface is level and even.
  • Lightly moisten the area before applying (avoid excess).
  • Fix any cracks or weak sections beforehand.

Mixing

Mixing POWERMAX 42.5 properly unlocks its superior workability and strength for projects like bridges or slabs. Use clean water and aggregates meeting standards, measured precisely with a bucket or wheelbarrow. Blend until uniform in color, adding minimal water to preserve strength and cohesion, ensuring top performance for high-strength or large pours.

  • Use impurity-free water (no salts or mud).
  • Select aggregates compliant with standards.
  • Measure cement, sand, and aggregates accurately.
  • Mix in a wheelbarrow or clean container.
  • Avoid overmixing to maintain strength.

Application

Applying POWERMAX 42.5 effectively leverages its high early strength and low heat benefits. Spread evenly, compact thoroughly, and finish smoothly for slabs, beams, or precast works. Skilled users should follow mix designs and cure well to reduce cracking and porosity, ensuring durable, high-performance concrete for both specialist and ordinary tasks.

  • Apply the mix evenly across the surface.
  • Compact concrete to remove air pockets.
  • Finish plaster or slabs with a smooth texture.
  • Stick to the specified mix design ratios.
  • Layer thick sections gradually if required.

Precautions

Safety and care optimize POWERMAX 42.5's performance. Wear protective gear to prevent irritation, store cement dry on pallets, and use it first-in, first-out. Cure for 7 days to avoid cracks, minimize water use, and dispose of waste properly. These steps ensure safe handling and maintain its strength and eco-friendly benefits for all projects.

  • Wear safety glasses and gloves during use.
  • Store cement on pallets 10cm off the floor.
  • Keep stacks dry and below 10 bags high.
  • Use cement in order of receipt (FIFO).
  • Cure concrete or plaster for at least 7 days.
