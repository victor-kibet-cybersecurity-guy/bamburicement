For residential foundations, we recommend Bamburi Tembo (32.5 MPa) as it offers the perfect balance of strength and cost-effectiveness. For commercial or heavy-load structures (multi-storey buildings, industrial projects), choose Bamburi PowerMax (42.5 MPa) for its superior compressive strength and low heat of hydration that reduces cracking in large pours. For coastal areas or aggressive soil conditions, Bamburi Duracem (42.5 MPa) provides excellent sulphate resistance.

Bamburi Fundi (22.5 MPa) is a specialized masonry cement designed for non-structural applications such as plastering, rendering, brick/block laying, and mortar works. It's formulated for excellent workability and smooth finishes

Bamburi Tembo (32.5 MPa) is a structural-grade all-purpose cement suitable for foundations, columns, beams, slabs, and general concrete works. It offers higher compressive strength and load-bearing capacity for building structures.

Key difference: Use Fundi for walls, plastering, and finishes. Use Tembo for anything structural that needs to bear weight. View the full comparison guide

Proper cement storage is essential to maintain quality and strength. Follow these best practices:

  • Keep it dry: Store in a dry, moisture-free area. Cement absorbs moisture from the air, which reduces its strength.
  • Raise off the ground: Store bags on wooden pallets or raised platforms (at least 15cm above the floor) to prevent ground moisture damage.
  • Keep away from walls: Maintain a gap between bags and walls to avoid dampness from condensation.
  • Use within 6 months: Cement has a shelf life of approximately 6 months (unopened). After this, it may lose strength.
  • Cover properly: Protect bags with tarpaulin or plastic sheeting if storing outdoors.
