Why Foundation Cement Matters

Your foundation is the most critical structural element of any building. It must withstand heavy loads, resist moisture and soil pressure, and provide a stable base for the entire structure. Using the correct cement type ensures:

  • Strength: Ability to support the weight of the building.
  • Durability: Resistance to cracking, sulfate attack, and moisture.
  • Setting Time: Proper curing without delays in construction.
  • Workability: Easy placement and compaction during pouring.

Tips for a Strong Foundation

1. Proper Mix Ratio

Use the correct cement-to-sand-to-aggregate ratio (e.g., 1:2:4 for standard foundations). Consult your structural engineer for specific requirements.

2. Adequate Curing

Keep the concrete moist for at least 7-14 days to achieve full strength. Cover with polythene or use wet curing methods.

3. Soil Testing

Know your soil type. In aggressive soils (high sulfate), use sulfate-resistant cement like Nguvu or Duracem.

4. Quality Water

Use clean, potable water for mixing. Avoid water with impurities that can weaken the concrete.
