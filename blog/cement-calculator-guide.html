How Many Bags of Cement Do I Need?

Use our cement calculator guide to estimate exactly how many bags of cement you need for foundations, slabs, plastering, and more. Includes formulas, examples, and quick reference tables for all your construction needs.

One of the most common questions we hear is "How many bags of cement do I need?" Whether you're building a house, pouring a slab, or plastering a wall, knowing how much cement to order is crucial for budgeting and avoiding waste.

This guide will show you how to calculate the number of cement bags needed for various construction projects. We'll cover foundations, slabs, plastering, and more

Cement Calculation Formulas

1. Concrete Slab or Foundation

Formula: Volume = Length

Cement Bags: 1 bag (50kg) covers approximately 0.035 m of concrete (1:2:4 mix ratio).

Example: A 10m

  • Volume = 10 12 m
  • Cement needed = 12 343 bags

2. Plastering

Formula: Area = Length

Cement Bags: 1 bag (50kg) covers approximately 8-10 m of plastering (15mm thickness).

Example: A wall 8m long and 3m high:

  • Area = 8 24 m
  • Cement needed = 24 2.7 bags (round up to 3 bags)

3. Block Laying (Mortar)

Formula: 1 bag of cement makes approximately 50-60 blocks (using standard 6" blocks with 1:6 mix).

Example: For 1,000 blocks:

  • Cement needed = 1000 18 bags

Step-by-Step Calculation Guide

Step 1: Determine Your Project Type

Identify what you're building: foundation, floor slab, wall (plastering or block laying), or other.

Step 2: Measure the Area

For walls: measure Length .

Step 3: Calculate Volume

Step 4: Convert to Bags

1 bag (50kg) covers approximately 0.035 m of concrete.

Bags = Volume

Example: 12 343 bags

For plastering, 1 bag covers 8-10 m at 15mm thickness.
For mortar (block laying), 1 bag makes 50-60 blocks.
For screeding, 1 bag covers 6-8 m at 25mm thickness.
