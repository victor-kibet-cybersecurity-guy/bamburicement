How to Identify Genuine Bamburi Cement

1. Check the Packaging

Genuine Bamburi cement has clear, high-quality printing. Look for sharp, professional branding and proper logos.

2. Verify the Batch Number

Every genuine bag has a unique batch number. This can be traced back to the manufacturing date and production line.

3. Check Manufacturing Date

Fresh cement is best. Check the manufacturing date

4. Feel the Cement

Genuine cement is a fine, smooth powder. It should flow like silk between your fingers. Gritty or lumpy = suspicious.

5. Buy from Authorized Dealers

Always buy from authorized Bamburi Cement distributors. They guarantee quality and authenticity.

6. Look for Official Markings

Genuine Bamburi cement has KEBS certification marks and official branding. Counterfeits often have misspelled words or poor printing.

Float Test: Throw a small handful of cement into water. Genuine cement should float briefly before sinking. Fake cement often sinks immediately or forms lumps. Feel Test: Genuine cement feels smooth and silky

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I tell if my cement is genuine?

Check the packaging quality, batch number, manufacturing date, and texture. Genuine Bamburi Cement has crisp printing, a unique batch number, a manufacturing date within 6 months, and is a fine, smooth powder.

Is cheaper cement worth it?

No. Cheaper cement is often substandard or counterfeit. It may seem like a saving initially, but it leads to structural failure, costly repairs, and safety risks that far outweigh the small savings.

What should I do if I bought fake cement?

Stop using it immediately. Contact Bamburi Cement to verify authenticity with the batch number. Report the seller to KEBS and consumer protection authorities. Consider legal action if you've suffered losses.

How do I report counterfeit cement?

Report to the Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS), the Anti-Counterfeit Authority, or contact Bamburi Cement directly. Keep your receipts and any evidence.
