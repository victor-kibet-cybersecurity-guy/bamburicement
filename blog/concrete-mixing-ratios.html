Concrete Mixing Ratios Explained

Get the perfect concrete mix every time. Learn the correct ratios of cement, sand, and aggregate for different applications. Understand 1:2:4, 1:3:6, and other mixing ratios for foundations, slabs, plastering, and more.

Getting the concrete mix right is one of the most important skills in construction. Use the wrong ratio, and your concrete could be too weak, crack easily, or fail prematurely. Use the right ratio, and you'll build something that lasts for decades.

This guide explains the different concrete mixing ratios, when to use each one, and how to mix them correctly. Whether you're building a foundation, pouring a slab, or plastering a wall, we've got you covered.

Understanding Concrete Mix Ratios

A concrete mix ratio is expressed as three numbers: Cement : Sand : Aggregate. For example, 1:2:4 means:

  • 1 part cement
  • 2 parts sand
  • 4 parts aggregate (crushed stone or gravel)

The amount of water added is also crucial. Too much water weakens the concrete. Too little makes it difficult to work with.

Common Concrete Mixing Ratios

1:2:4 Foundation & Slabs

1 part cement : 2 parts sand : 4 parts aggregate
The most common general-purpose mix. Used for foundations, floor slabs, columns, and beams. Provides good strength for most residential projects.

1:3:6 Heavy Foundation

1 part cement : 3 parts sand : 6 parts aggregate
A leaner mix used for large foundations, mass concrete, and less critical structural elements. More economical but lower strength than 1:2:4.

1:1.5:3 High-Strength

1 part cement : 1.5 parts sand : 3 parts aggregate
A high-strength mix used for beams, columns, high-rise buildings, and heavy structural elements. Provides maximum compressive strength.

1:4:8 Block Laying

1 part cement : 4 parts sand : 8 parts aggregate
A lean mix used for making concrete blocks, paving, and other non-critical applications. Economical and easy to work with.

1:3 Plastering

1 part cement : 3 parts sand
A mortar mix for plastering, rendering, and brick/block laying. No aggregate is used. Provides a smooth, workable finish.

1:4 Mortar

1 part cement : 4 parts sand
A leaner mortar mix used for general brick/block laying. More economical than 1:3 but lower strength.

How to Mix Concrete

1

Measure Materials

Use a measuring container (bucket or wheelbarrow). Measure cement, sand, and aggregate according to your chosen ratio.

2

Dry Mix

Mix the dry ingredients (cement, sand, aggregate) thoroughly until they are a uniform color. This ensures even distribution.

3

Add Water

Add clean water gradually while mixing. The right amount is critical

4

Mix Thoroughly

Continue mixing until all materials are combined and the mix is uniform. There should be no dry patches or lumps.

Water-to-Cement Ratio

The water-to-cement ratio is the most critical factor in concrete strength. Use the minimum water needed to make the mix workable. Too much water weakens the concrete significantly.

A general rule of thumb is to use about 5-7 liters of water per 50kg bag of cement. However, the exact amount depends on the moisture content of your sand and aggregate.

Mixing Tips for Best Results

  • Use clean materials: Sand and aggregate should be free from dirt, clay, and organic matter.
  • Measure accurately: Use a bucket or wheelbarrow to measure materials. Consistency is key.
  • Mix in the right order: Always dry mix first, then add water gradually.
  • Don't overmix: Overmixing can cause the concrete to segregate and lose strength.
  • Use the mix quickly: Concrete starts to set within 30-60 minutes. Don't mix more than you can use.
  • Cure properly: Keep the concrete moist for at least 7 days after pouring.

  • Too much water: Weakens the concrete significantly.
  • Poor measurement: Inconsistent ratios lead to inconsistent strength.
  • Dirty materials: Contaminants weaken the concrete and prevent proper bonding.
  • Not curing: Concrete needs moisture to reach its full strength.
  • Using the wrong cement: Different projects need different cement types.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best concrete mix for a foundation?

1:2:4 (cement:sand:aggregate) is the most common and reliable mix for residential foundations. For larger buildings, use 1:1.5:3.

What is the best concrete mix for a slab?

1:2:4 is the standard mix for floor slabs. It provides good strength and workability for most residential applications.

What is the best mortar mix for plastering?

1:3 (cement:sand) is the standard plastering mix. For a smoother finish, you can use 1:2, but this is more expensive.

How much water do I add to concrete?

Use approximately 5–7 litres of water per 50 kg bag of cement. The exact amount depends on the moisture content of your sand and aggregate.

What is the difference between mortar and concrete?

Mortar is a mix of cement, sand, and water used for binding bricks or blocks. Concrete includes aggregate (stone or gravel) and is used for structural elements like foundations, slabs, and columns.
