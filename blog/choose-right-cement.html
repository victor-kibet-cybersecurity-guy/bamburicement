Key Factors to Consider When Choosing Cement

1. Choose the Right Type of Cement

Fundi (22.5 MPa): Ideal for plastering, rendering, and light masonry where smooth finishing is required.
Tembo (32.5 MPa): Popular for masonry and block making due to its workability.
Nguvu (32.5 MPa): Used for general construction, including mortar and block laying.
PowerMax (42.5 MPa): Premium high-strength for heavy structural applications.
Duracem (42.5 MPa): Sulphate-resistant for aggressive environments.
PowerPlus (42.5 MPa): High-strength OPC for large-scale projects.
Powercrete (52.5 MPa): Ultra-high strength for the most demanding applications.

2. Match the Grade to the Structural Element

Higher-grade cement isn't always better for every part of the structure. Use lower grades for non-structural work like masonry and plastering. Use higher grades for heavy load-bearing areas like high-rise slabs and columns.

3. Consider Your Environmental Exposure

Coastal Regions & Marine Areas: Concrete is vulnerable to chloride and sulfate attacks. Choose Duracem.
Hot & Dry Climates: The hydration process generates heat which can cause cracks. Choose cement with low heat of hydration.
High Water Tables: If building in areas with high moisture or soil contact, consider Duracem for sulphate resistance.

4. Quality and Sourcing

Always check the manufacturing date. Cement degrades in quality and strength the longer it sits. Fresh cement (preferably manufactured within the last 3 months) is vital for maximum structural integrity.

High-quality cement should feel very smooth and cool to the touch. If you notice hard lumps or a gritty feel, the cement has absorbed moisture and should be rejected.

Always mix your cement with clean water and good sand. Wet the concrete for at least 7 days after pouring it. This helps the cement reach its true strength!

Proper curing is essential for achieving the maximum strength and durability of your concrete.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between 32.5R and 32.5N cement?

32.5N: This is the most commonly used cement with ordinary early strength. It is more suitable where high early strength is not required for construction. The 'N' in the cement class denotes normal strength.
32.5R: This type of cement is used where high early strength is required for construction.

Which Bamburi Cement is best for plastering?

Bamburi Fundi is the best choice for plastering. It is a specialized masonry cement formulated for optimal workability, smooth finishes, and excellent bonding.

Which Bamburi Cement is best for foundations?

For residential foundations, Bamburi Tembo is recommended. For commercial or heavy-load structures, Bamburi PowerMax or Bamburi Nguvu are better choices.

Can I use Tembo for plastering?

While Tembo can be used for plastering, Fundi is specifically formulated for this purpose and delivers better results with smoother finishes and easier application.
