Bamburi Cement Comparison

Bamburi cement types differ in their strength and the best tasks they do. Fundi is for soft jobs like plastering. Tembo and Nguvu do general building and bricklaying. PowerMax is the strongest type for heavy jobs like beams and slabs. This guide breaks down the differences so you can choose the right one.

Choosing the right Bamburi cement for your project can be confusing. With multiple products available, it's important to understand the differences between them. This guide compares Fundi, Tembo, Nguvu, and PowerMax

Compressive strength is measured in Megapascals (MPa). It tells you how much weight the hardened concrete can hold after 28 days. The higher the number, the stronger the cement.

Breaking Down the Cement Types

1. Bamburi Fundi (22.5 MPa)

Masonry Cement Best for Plastering

Price: KSh 580 per 50kg bag
Strength: 22.5 MPa
Type: Masonry Cement

This is a masonry cement made just for plastering, screeds (smooth floor layers), and laying bricks. It acts like glue for bricks but does not hold heavy weight. It is smooth and easy to spread for workers. Think of it like buttering toast.

Best for: Plastering, rendering, brick/block laying, floor screeding, and repair works.

Learn more about Fundi

2. Bamburi Tembo (32.5 MPa)

All-Purpose Cement General Construction

Price: KSh 620 per 50kg bag
Strength: 32.5 MPa
Type: All-Purpose Cement

This is a general-purpose cement. It is great for mixing concrete for small foundations, making cement blocks, and everyday house projects. It is a reliable choice for standard family homes. It is popular for masonry and block making due to its workability.

Best for: Foundations, columns, beams, slabs, bridges, and water-retaining structures.

Learn more about Tembo

3. Bamburi Nguvu (32.5 MPa)

High-Strength Cement Faster Setting

Price: KSh 680 per 50kg bag
Strength: 32.5 MPa
Type: High-Strength Cement

This cement has the same strength rating as Tembo but is often used for bigger structural jobs like columns and floor slabs. It helps builders make strong concrete that lasts. It hardens a bit faster and shrinks less, reducing cracks in walls.

Best for: Heavy-duty foundations, columns, beams, suspended slabs, water-retaining structures, and bridges.

Learn more about Nguvu

4. Bamburi PowerMax (42.5 MPa)

Premium Cement Highest Strength

Price: KSh 750 per 50kg bag
Strength: 42.5 MPa
Type: Premium Cement

This is a premium, high-strength cement. The number 42.5 means it is much harder than Tembo and Nguvu. Builders use PowerMax for big, tough jobs that take a lot of weight. This includes bridges, multi-story buildings, and heavy concrete slabs. It is highly efficient and lets you use fewer bags per project.

Best for: High-rise construction, bridges, dams, reservoirs, massive beams and columns, and soil stabilization.

Learn more about PowerMax

How to Choose the Right One

  • For plastering and finishing walls: Use Fundi
  • For basic walls and foundations: Use Tembo or Nguvu
  • For heavy structures, beams, and slabs: Use PowerMax
  • If you need faster setting: Choose Nguvu

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Bamburi cement for plastering?

Bamburi Fundi is ideal for plastering, rendering, and light masonry where smooth finishing is required. It is specifically formulated for non-structural applications like plastering and block laying.

What is the difference between 32.5R and 32.5N cement?

32.5N: This is the most commonly used cement with ordinary early strength. It is more suitable where high early strength is not required for construction. The 'N' in the cement class denotes normal strength.
32.5R: This type of cement is used where high early strength is required for construction.

What is the difference between 42.5N and 42.5R cement?

Both 42.5N and 42.5R cement reach the same minimum compressive strength of 42.5 MPa after 28 days. The difference lies in the speed of their strength development:
42.5N: Normal early strength. Ideal for general structural work like columns, slabs, and beams.
42.5R: Rapid early strength. Typically used in colder weather or for heavy-duty projects like water projects and precast concrete.

Which is the strongest cement in Kenya?

The strongest cement available in Kenya is Bamburi Powercrete (52.5 strength grade). It is an ultra-high-strength structural cement specifically formulated for heavy-duty engineering works, high-rise buildings, and pre-stressed concrete structures.

Can I use Tembo for plastering?

While Tembo can be used for plastering, Fundi is specifically formulated for this purpose and delivers better results with smoother finishes and easier application.
