Using Bamburi Nguvu Cement

Surface Preparation

For optimal results with Bamburi Nguvu Cement, proper surface preparation is crucial. Ensure the substrate is stable and leveled. Remove any loose materials or existing weak concrete. Eliminate contaminants like dust or grease using a wire brush or pressure wash. This prepares the surface for a strong bond, ensuring durable and lasting structural work.

Versatile and strong

  • Clear the surface of dust, dirt, and loose material.
  • Remove any grease or oil stains from the area.
  • Lightly dampen the surface for plastering (avoid soaking).
  • Repair cracks or weak spots before starting.
  • Ensure the base is stable for structural tasks.

Choose Bamburi Nguvu cement for fast-setting, strong, and sulphate-resistant concrete

Mixing

Mixing NGUVU cement correctly is vital for its high early strength and workability. Use clean water and standard-compliant aggregates, measured accurately with a bucket or wheelbarrow. Blend thoroughly until the mix is uniform in color, using minimal water to maintain strength. This ensures top performance for concrete or mortar applications.

  • Use clean water free of salts or impurities.
  • Choose aggregates meeting relevant standards.
  • Measure cement, sand, and aggregates precisely.
  • Stir until the mix is a consistent color.
  • Avoid overmixing to preserve strength.

Application

Applying NGUVU cement effectively maximizes its versatility for beams, blocks, or plaster. Skilled users should spread the mix evenly, compact concrete well, and finish smoothly for professional results. Follow mix designs and cure properly to leverage its high strength and sulphate resistance, ensuring lasting performance across structural and precast uses.

  • Spread the mix evenly over the surface.
  • Compact concrete to eliminate air voids.
  • Adhere to the recommended mix proportions.
  • Apply in stages for thicker sections if needed.
  • Start curing soon after the mix sets.

Precautions

Safety and care enhance NGUVU cement's effectiveness. Wear protective gear to avoid irritation, store cement dry on pallets, and use it first-in, first-out. Cure for 7 days to prevent cracks, use minimal water, and dispose of waste responsibly. These steps ensure safe handling and maintain the cement's quality for strong, lasting construction.

  • Wear safety glasses and gloves when working.
  • Store cement 10cm above the floor on pallets.
  • Keep stacks below 10 bags in a dry space.
  • Use cement in order of arrival.
  • Cure concrete or plaster for at least 7 days.
